Luck Rating Analysis
Regression Candidates

KenPom's Luck metric measures the gap between actual wins and expected wins based on efficiency. Teams with very positive luck ratings have outperformed their metrics — and may regress in March. Teams with negative luck may be better than their record shows.

⚠️ HIGH LUCK — Possible Regression

These teams won more than their efficiency metrics predicted — they may have been "lucky" in close games. Fade in tight spots.

✅ NEGATIVE LUCK — Undervalued

These teams won fewer games than their efficiency deserved — they're better than their record suggests. Potential overlooks.

Key context: Luck doesn't mean bad team — it means close games broke a certain way. Florida (−0.036 luck) is a 1-seed that actually underperformed its metrics. That means they're probably even better than their wins suggest. Meanwhile McNeese (+0.084 luck) is an 12-seed that overperformed significantly — this is a team to potentially fade in tight spots.
